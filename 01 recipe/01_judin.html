Mexican Zucchini Boats

yield: 8 boats prep time: 25 minutes cook time: 30 minutes total time: 55 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 large zucchini
  • 1 (15 ounce) can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup salsa (Store bought, or try mango salsa for an extra kick)
  • 1 red bell pepper, cored and diced
  • 1/2 red onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, cored and diced
  • olive oil
  • 2 tsp. cumin
  • 1 tsp. chili powder
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar/monterey jack cheese
  • Guacamole (Optional, for added flavor)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 400F. Grease a 9x13" casserole dish then set aside.
  2. Slice each zucchini in half lengthwise. Using a melon baller or metal teaspoon, hollow out the center of each zucchini. Lightly brush the tops of the zucchini with one teaspoon of olive oil then place them skin side down in the casserole dish.
  3. Next warm the tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and the peppers and cook for 2-3 minutes. Then add the rice, corn, and beans along with the salsa, chili powder and cumin. If you want to amp up the flavor of your boats, try this homemade mix for taco seasoning instead. Stir everything together and continue to cook for about 5 minutes then remove the skillet from the heat and set aside.
  4. Spoon the filling inside of each zucchini until they are all full. Sprinkle each one with cheese then arrange them in the dish and cover with foil.
  5. Bake in the oven for 25 minutes then remove the foil and set the oven to broil. Cook them for 5 more minutes, until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  6. Allow them to cool for 5-10 minutes then top with fresh cilantro and serve. Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
